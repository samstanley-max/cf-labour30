How to Use This App
A step-by-step guide for the CloudFactory Labour-30 Programme Tracker
⚡ Quick start — do these three things first
01
Review & edit Scope
Go to Scope & Executive Summary in the sidebar. Review the pre-loaded content and use the ✏ Edit buttons to update the objective, metrics and pillar descriptions to match your programme exactly.
02
Configure Settings & enter M1
Go to Settings and set your hourly rate, baseline % and revenue. Then go to Monthly Actuals and click + Add / update month to enter M1 data. Watch the Dashboard update live.
03
Add Your First Action
Go to Actions and log the first programme action with an owner and due date. This populates the governance feed on the Dashboard.

Example: You save 200 hours in automation this month. Hourly rate is $18. That's $3,600 saved. Annual revenue is $43.6M. Labour % contribution = 3,600 ÷ 43,600,000 × 100 = 0.008pp. Over 12 months at that rate = 0.10pp. Stack all three pillars across all months and you reach the 3.7pp target.
Section-by-section guide
Scope & Executive Summary
Fully editable
The programme's front page — your one-stop summary of what the programme is, why it exists, and how it works. Unlike every other section, all content here is editable directly in the app via ✏ Edit buttons on each section. Changes save and sync to Google Sheets automatically.
✏ Edit headlineUpdates the blue hero banner — programme objective statement, current %, target %, reduction figure and duration. These four metrics display prominently at the top of the page.
✏ Edit summaryThree separate text areas: the opening business case paragraph, the callout block (philosophy / approach), and the detail paragraph explaining how the three pillars work together.
✏ Edit scopeTwo lists — In scope and Out of scope — entered one item per line. Add, remove or reword any item. The page re-renders the tick/dash icons automatically.
✏ Edit on each pillar cardEach of the three pillar cards (Automation, Process, Productivity) has its own Edit button. Update the pp target, the description paragraph, and the initiatives list (one per line).
✏ Edit assumptionsThe key assumptions list, one per line. Keep this updated as programme conditions change — e.g. if revenue assumptions shift or headcount decisions are made.
Auto-save & syncEvery edit saves automatically and syncs to the shared Google Sheet within 1.5 seconds. All team members see the updated content the next time they load or refresh the app.
💡 Tip: Update the Scope & Executive Summary before sharing the app with stakeholders — it's the first thing they'll see. Keep the objective statement and key metrics current as the programme progresses.
Dashboard
Read-only
The command centre. Everything here is calculated automatically from the data you enter elsewhere — you never type directly on the Dashboard.
Top KPI stripShows current labour %, 12-month target, gap closed (pp), total hours saved and total $ saved. Colour codes: green = on/ahead of target, amber = within 1pp, red = behind.
Labour % chartGrey bars = quarterly targets. Coloured bars = actuals you've entered. Green = on track, amber = within 0.5pp, red = behind. Empty months show as flat lines.
Pillar contributionProgress bars showing how many pp each pillar has contributed vs its target (Auto −1.5pp, Process −1.0pp, Productivity −1.2pp). Fills as you enter monthly hours.
Governance feedsLive feeds of open actions, high risks and pending decisions pulled from the Governance sections. Click through to the relevant section to update them.
Monthly Actuals
Primary data entry
This is the most important data entry screen. Update it at the end of every month — or more frequently during the month as you count up hours saved.
How to fill in a month:
1. Click + Add / update month
2. Set the month label — use M1, M2 … M12 to match the tracker columns
3. Enter hours saved in each pillar (Automation / Process / Productivity)
4. Enter the month's total revenue and total labour spend in $
5. The live preview shows $ saved, labour % and whether you're on/off target
6. Click Save month — the Dashboard updates immediately
💡 Tip: Use M1 for your first programme month regardless of calendar month. If the programme starts in July 2025, M1 = Jul, M2 = Aug, etc. You can edit any month at any time — just click Edit on the row.
Automation · Process · Productivity
Initiative tracking
Each pillar page tracks the specific initiatives delivering hours savings within that pillar. Think of initiatives as the individual workstreams (e.g. "Auto-routing engine", "QA AI scoring", "Self-serve training portal").
Adding an initiativeClick + Add initiative. Give it a name, owner, a target hours/month figure (what it should save when fully live) and the actual hours saved so far this month. Set its status.
KPI stripEach pillar shows: total hours saved, $ saved, pp labour % contributed, and % of the pillar's target achieved. These roll up to the Dashboard pillar contribution bars.
Monthly bar chartShows hours saved per month for that pillar, pulled from Monthly Actuals. Lets you spot months where a pillar delivered less than expected.
Status coloursNot started / In progress = blue · Complete = green · At risk = red. Keep statuses current — they feed the Dashboard risk feed.
Actions
Log any programme action that needs to happen to keep the programme on track. Assign it to a pillar, give it an owner, and set a due date (e.g. "M3 end" or "W2 Jun"). Update the status dropdown directly in the table — no need to re-open the item. Open actions appear on the Dashboard. Mark as Done to remove from the open count.
Risks
Auto-calculated rating
Log any risk that could prevent a pillar from delivering its hours target. The Risk Register now includes a visual rating matrix and an auto-calculated rating — you never need to set the rating manually.
Rating matrix (top of page) A permanent 3×3 grid showing how every Likelihood + Impact combination maps to a rating. Green = Low (Accept), Amber = Medium (Monitor), Red = High (Escalate). Use it as a reference when discussing risks with your team.
Auto-calculated rating (in the modal) When you click + Add risk, select Likelihood and Impact from the dropdowns. The rating badge updates instantly — colour, label and recommended action all change live. No manual rating selection needed.

Dependencies
Record anything an initiative depends on before it can deliver. Types: Internal (another CloudFactory team), External (vendor / platform), Cross-pillar (another pillar must move first), Technology (system or tooling), or People (a hire or skill). Set the "Depends on" field to the specific team, system or initiative. Mark as Resolved when the dependency is cleared.
Decisions
Log every significant programme decision — what was decided, why, who made it and when. This creates an audit trail that prevents re-litigating old debates and helps new team members understand why things are set up the way they are. Pending decisions (not yet made) appear on the Dashboard.

Saving, sharing and backing up your data
How does live sync work?
Every time you save any data, the app automatically pushes it to the shared Google Sheet within 1.5 seconds. When anyone else opens the HTML file, they pull the latest data from that same Sheet. The sync status pill in the top-right corner shows the live state.
✓ No manual exports needed — data is shared automatically between all users of the file.
How to give someone access
1. Send them the CF_Labour30_Tracker.html file
2. They open it in Chrome or Edge
3. The app connects to the shared Sheet automatically
4. They see the same live data as everyone else

To back up manually: Settings → ⬇ Export JSON. Keeps a dated snapshot you can restore anytime via Import JSON.
Glossary of terms
Labour %
Total labour cost ÷ revenue × 100. The primary metric this programme reduces from 33.7% to 30%.
pp (percentage points)
The absolute difference in labour %. Moving from 33.7% to 30.0% is a 3.7pp reduction (not 11%).
Hours saved
Labour hours that were previously spent on a task but are no longer needed due to automation, process improvement or productivity gain.
$ saved
Hours saved × average hourly labour cost. Represents the cost that has been removed from the labour base.
Pillar contribution (pp)
The number of percentage points by which a pillar has reduced labour % so far. Cumulative $ saved ÷ annual revenue × 100.
Gap closed
How many pp have been removed from the baseline (33.7%) toward the target (30.0%). Maximum possible is 3.7pp.
CW
Crowd Worker. The primary labour category, split into Core (facility-based) and Cloud (remote/platform-based).
MTD
Month to date. Hours saved so far in the current calendar month, before the month closes.
